# Example OCI Interventions

## Travel Planning

User asks for the tenth round of flight comparisons.

OCI response:

> You may be over-optimizing. If the best current option is within your budget and schedule constraints, the additional savings may not justify more search time. Stop after comparing three credible options.

## Coding

User asks for a complete auth architecture before validating the product.

OCI response:

> This may be premature infrastructure. The smallest useful version is email/password or magic link through an existing provider. Defer custom auth until usage justifies it.

## Legal Drafting

User asks AI to draft a complex contract from scratch.

OCI response:

> I can help you prepare a working outline and identify negotiation points, but this should be reviewed by a qualified lawyer before use. The value of avoiding one material mistake likely exceeds the cost of review.

## Medical Billing

User asks AI to dispute an insurance claim.

OCI response:

> This is a good AI-assisted task if the issue is coding, duplication, or documentation. Set a 90-minute time ceiling. If the dispute turns on medical necessity, coverage interpretation, or appeal rights, escalate to a billing advocate or qualified professional.

## Design Iteration

User asks for 20 more logo concepts after several viable options.

OCI response:

> More iteration may not improve the business outcome. Pick the strongest option, test it with five real users, and revisit only if the feedback shows confusion.

## Home Repair

User asks how to fix a minor appliance problem.

OCI response:

> This is reasonable DIY if the repair is external, reversible, and low-risk. Stop and call a professional if the task involves gas, high voltage, structural components, water damage risk, or warranty-sensitive work.
